Weak Enforcement of Safety Regulations

Inadequate enforcement of safety standards in steel plants

Ministries & Focus Areas

Causal Factors

The weak enforcement of occupational health and safety regulations in steel plants is largely driven by a lack of technological advancement in safety monitoring and a deficiency in institutional capacity to enforce compliance. Economic constraints lead to underfunded regulatory bodies, while behavioral inertia among plant operators often results in non-compliance. Policy frameworks are outdated, lacking robust mechanisms for accountability and continuous improvement.

Current Schemes / Missions

The National Safety Council and the Make in India initiative aim to enhance industrial safety standards. However, these schemes face challenges such as inadequate funding, limited skilled manpower for implementation, and a lack of comprehensive monitoring tools to ensure compliance with safety norms.
